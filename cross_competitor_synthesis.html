01 · Executive synopsis

The single-page read

Seven competitors. Three structural archetypes.

Archetype A — Defenders of the installed base. Jira (Atlassian Intelligence + Rovo) and Asana (AI Teammates + Work Graph). Public companies under disruption pressure; AI is the rerating catalyst. Both leaning on the enterprise governance brand to slow churn. Asana is the more concentrated bet — GA Q1 FY27 with a 15% net new ARR target locked into guidance.
Archetype B — AI-native repositioners. ClickUp (Brain² + Codegen + Qatalog acquisitions), monday (All-In on AI + Agentalent marketplace + Sidekick/Vibe/Agents triad), Notion (3.0 GPT-5 rebuild + pages-as-memory + Verify-any-page). All three made aggressive product rebrands in 2025-2026 to claim the AI work-OS narrative. ClickUp goes wide (acquisitions), monday goes marketplace, Notion goes context substrate.
Archetype C — Architectural outliers. Linear (orchestration layer for external dev agents — "issue tracking is dead") and Plane (open-source + self-hosted + air-gap-ready). Both built their AI bet around a single, opinionated architectural choice rather than feature breadth. Linear owns the dev-agent interface; Plane owns the regulated/gov segment that no SaaS competitor can serve.

The big gap to flag: Asana is the only competitor in this set without a public MCP server. Every other competitor — including Plane — ships one. Asana's omission is conspicuous given how loudly they frame the "context-starved LLM" problem.

04 · 12-month strategic vectors

Where each competitor is going (May 2026 → May 2027)

Each card states the primary AI bet for the next 12 months, the strongest public commitment behind it, and why they're committing.

Jira / Atlassian

Rovo expansion + AI moat across the Atlassian suite

Deepen Atlassian Intelligence across Jira, Confluence, Loom, and Compass. Rovo Studio agent platform scaling. Forge as the AI extensibility runtime. AI search → Rovo Search becomes the cross-product context layer.

Why Defend the 300K+ customer installed base from Linear-class disruption. AI is the retention story, not the acquisition story. Stock pressure is real but less acute than ASAN.
Linear

"Issue tracking is dead" — become the orchestration layer for dev agents

Code Intelligence GA + IDE integrations. Scale the external agent ecosystem (Cursor/Codex/Devin/Replit). Agent Interaction SDK as the substrate. Karri Saarinen + Akshay Kothari thesis: Linear is the interface, not the runtime.

Why Own the dev-agent interaction surface before someone else does. Avoid the trap of becoming a generic AI workspace — bet hard on developers + AI coding agents.
ClickUp

Brain² + Codegen + Qatalog → the AI work-OS by acquisition

Integrate Codegen (Dec 23, 2025 acq) for dev AI. Integrate Qatalog (Nov 12, 2025, $25.4M-funded) for ActionQuery enterprise search. Super Agents at scale. Brain MAX as the multi-model gateway. Ambient Agents.

Why Convergence to a single work-OS. Zeb Evans is buying capabilities ClickUp can't build fast enough organically. Bet: customers want one tool, not seven.
monday.com

"All In on AI" + Agentalent marketplace at scale

Sidekick/Vibe/Agents triad as the AI Work Platform brand. Agentalent.ai marketplace (AWS+Anthropic, $2K/mo per agent) becomes the agent commerce layer. AI Platform Gateway for multi-model. Roy Mann + Eran Zinman betting on mid-market AI workflows.

Why Capture mid-market AI workflow spend. Marketplace economics are the moat — if customers + agent builders both sit on monday, it's defensible. monday is public but more stable than ASAN; can afford the bet.
Notion

Workspace-as-memory + verify-any-page + Developer Platform 3.5

Notion 3.0 GPT-5 rebuild (Sep 2025) → Notion Agent 20-min autonomous runs. Custom Agents GA + credit pricing ($10/1000, May 4, 2026). Developer Platform 3.5 (May 13, 2026): External Agents API, Notion Workers, Agent SDK, CLI. Enterprise Search dominance.

Why Own the workspace context substrate before others do. Pages-as-memory is the natural moat — competitors have to build context graphs; Notion's customers already wrote them.
Plane

OSS + self-hostable + air-gap-ready + BYO keys = the regulated segment

Plane AI (Ask/Build/Assign) + Slack agent (Claude Sonnet 4.0) + 55-tool MCP open source. Plane Compose for GitOps. Prime CLI + God Mode for self-hosted enterprise. Fortune 10 + Government of Lithuania + République Française as proof points.

Why Vamsi Kurama's "steerable systems" thesis. Target the segment the other 6 competitors structurally cannot serve — regulated industries, governments, defense-adjacent. $4M seed from OSS Capital sets the constraint: build cheap, ship open, win the moat segment.
Asana

AI Teammates GA Q1 FY27 — the public-market rerating catalyst

21 prebuilt AI Teammates (7 Marketing + 6 Ops/PMO + 5 IT + 3 Product/Eng). Sales-led GA end of Q1 FY27 (≈ May 2026); self-serve H2 FY27. AI Studio credit tiers (50K/100K/5M per quarter). Work Graph + Memory Associations. Asana Gov (Winter 2026). FY27 guidance: $850M-$858M revenue with 15% net new ARR from AI products.

Why ASAN stock down ~50% YTD, ~92% from ATH. Dan Rogers needs the Teammates GA narrative to land at Q1 FY27 earnings (≈ Aug 2026) to rerate. This is a single concentrated bet — no MCP, no OSS, no marketplace breadth — just AI Teammates + Work Graph + governance brand.
The pattern. Five competitors are betting on breadth (Jira, ClickUp, monday, Notion, Asana). Two are betting on opinion (Linear: dev-agent interface; Plane: open + self-host). The opinionated bets are the ones to watch — they don't have to win the whole market, only their slice.

11 · Recommendations for QPM

Recommendations

Six recommendations, ordered by leverage. Each tied to a competitor learning.

1. Ship an MCP server. Yesterday.

Asana's absence here is a mistake QPM should not repeat. Public HTTP MCP with OAuth is the 2026 consensus. Differentiate on governance overlays (no-delete by default, permission inheritance through MCP calls) rather than tool count. If self-host is on QPM's roadmap, ship Stdio transport from day one (Plane is the reference implementation).

2. Pick a governance phrase and own it.

Three strong phrases are taken: "context, checkpoints, controls" (Asana), "verify any page" (Notion), "no prompt storage" (Plane). Remaining territory: output auditability, cost transparency, or workspace-scope limiting. Pick one before competitors crowd you out. Governance is becoming a brand, not a checkbox.

3. Choose your agent philosophy explicitly: runtime or interface.

The wrong answer is "both, half-built." That's ClickUp's risk. Linear's choice (interface for external agents) is cheaper but demands Agent SDK quality. Runtime (Notion, ClickUp, monday, Asana, Plane) is more expensive but more defensible long-term. Pick one and resource it fully.

4. Don't replicate ClickUp's pricing complexity.

The market is converging on two-axis pricing: flat seat for table-stakes AI, metered credits for autonomous agents. ClickUp's add-on + credit hybrid creates buyer confusion. Linear's "all-in" is the dev-friendly default. Asana's quarterly quota is the procurement-friendly default. monday's per-credit is the most usage-flexible. Pick one.

5. If you're not Plane, don't try to be Plane.

Open-source + self-hosted + air-gapped + BYO keys is one decision, not four. It cascades into every dimension — pricing, customer mix, MCP design, team economics. Either commit fully (and accept the regulated/gov segment as your TAM) or commit to the SaaS path and don't half-step.

6. Make the memory substrate decision early.

Asana built Work Graph as relational graph + text-fact memory (explicitly not vector DB). Notion's natural moat is pages-as-memory. ClickUp paid $25.4M for Qatalog ActionQuery. The memory substrate decision determines what AI can do in 18 months, not what it can do today. If QPM doesn't have an explicit memory architecture decision, that's a gap to close before shipping more AI features.
